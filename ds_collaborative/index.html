BIDS thunder talk

Data Science and Ecological Data

Falk Schuetzenmeister



September 4, 2014

Web Application Developer Geospatial Innovation Facility College of Natural Resources

ecoengine.berkeley.edu

A RESTful API serving UC Berkeley's natural history data.

  • EVENT DATA (defined by a locality and time)
    • 5 million museum specimen across all taxa (about 10 to 20 million by the end of the project)
    • a collection of about 60,000 photos reflecting ecological change in California
    • sensor data from UC California's field station system
    • historic vegetation survey data
  • CONTINOUS SURFACES (several TB, mostly geospatial rasters)
    • climate data (daily, historical, observed, projected)
    • landuse, land ownership, political
    • ...


Time and location as organizing principles

  • Species distribution modeling and niche theory
  • Global change as concern
  • Space and time as smallest common denominator
  • BUT: Ecological data is removed from its context, space and time are ways to feed back context.
  • Museum specimen hold more information (DNA).
  • How to create resources that make recontextualization of ecological data possible?
  • Please help!!!

Conceptual model

Example: data extraction and aggregation


  • Common task in ecological research is to enrich an occurrence of a species with environmental data obtained from geospatial layers
    • A small amount of data gets retrieved from large data sets.
    • Aggregation of data is very common, e.g. monthly coldest temperature, or coldest month of the year, etc.

Example: the application model

  • Very different uses for the data: scientific and non-scientific.
    • Web applications for research, citizen science, education, land management, and data journalism
    • Desktop GIS software
    • Scientific computing frameworks such as rOpenSci
    Example: Wieslander Vegetation Type Mapping

Domain specific problems

  • Data is messy and biased:
    • biases: towards interest of collectors/curators, accessibility roads/settlements, changing research interests
    • data collection by many different people over more then 100 years
    • no leading theory, high number of fields, normalization and standardization rudimentary
  • Too little data science and software engineering expertise:
    • What needs to be done to achieve a good requirement management related to the cutting edge of research?
  • How to represent fundamental ecological thinking in our data models?
    • Are RMDBS really appropriate for ecological data?
  • Only few theoretical approaches that can be translated in numerical algorithms.
    • species distribution modeling
    • genetics as a promising case

Resulting questions: Data science as web service?


Our data is not outragously big BUT we have to process our data within the request-response-cycle on the web (ideally within one second).

What data science technologies can be applied?

The work flows in our field need to be studied more thoroughly

What functionality can be effectively delivered by a RESTful API?

My goals at BIDS

  • Promoting RESTful APIs as an important building block in reproducible science.
  • Create a new way to think about very heterogenous and distributed ecological data: Putting ecology back into ecological data.
  • Identify resources and work flows that can be a foundation for a virtual workbench of ecological science based on a whole array of RESTful APIs
  • Products
    • Best Practices for interoperability of RESTful API's.
    • Performance and Persistence of RESTful API's.
    • Building User Communities around Web Services and API's.
  • I hope to develop the Ecoengine as a role model for a RESTful data service. I would like to collaborate with other fellows to apply my experience to another domain.
